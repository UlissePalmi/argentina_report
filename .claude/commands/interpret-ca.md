Read the files `output/imf_current_account.csv`, `output/indec_trade.csv`, and `output/bcra_reserves.csv`, then write a concise analytical interpretation of Argentina's external / dollar situation following these rules:

## Structure
1. **Current account headline** — state the latest quarter's CA balance in USD billions, whether it is a surplus or deficit, and how it compares to the prior quarter and prior year.
2. **Trade balance driver** — look at the last 6 months of exports and imports. Is the CA surplus/deficit driven by goods trade or something else? Is the trend improving or deteriorating?
3. **Reserves trajectory** — state the current gross reserves level, the change over the last 6 months, and whether the trend is accumulation or drawdown.
4. **Seasonal or cyclical flags** — Argentina's CA is highly seasonal (agro exports peak Q2). Note if the current reading is distorted by seasonality.
5. **One-sentence bottom line** — plain English verdict on whether Argentina is gaining or losing dollars and whether the external position is sustainable.

## Thresholds to flag explicitly
- CA deficit above $3bn/quarter: external pressure, watch reserves
- CA surplus above $2bn/quarter: favorable, but check if driven by import compression
- Reserves below $25bn gross: critical warning
- Reserves change worse than -$2bn in a single month: acute stress signal
- Trade surplus below $500m/month: deteriorating goods balance

## Tone
Be analytical, not descriptive. Always anchor numbers in context (e.g. "reserves are at X, the lowest/highest since Y"). Flag if the situation is improving or deteriorating with a clear directional call. Keep it under 200 words.
