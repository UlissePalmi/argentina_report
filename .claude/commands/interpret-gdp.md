Read the file `output/gdp_components.csv` and `output/wb_gdp_growth.csv`, then write a concise analytical interpretation of Argentina's GDP situation following these rules:

## Structure
1. **Headline number** — state the latest quarter's YoY growth rate and whether it beat or missed the prior quarter.
2. **Demand breakdown** — for each component (C, G, I, X, M), note whether it contributed positively or negatively to growth and flag any that moved by more than 5pp quarter-on-quarter.
3. **Trend assessment** — look at the last 4 quarters and state whether the economy is accelerating, decelerating, or bottoming out. Use the actual numbers.
4. **Key risk or tailwind** — identify the single most important dynamic (e.g. investment collapse, export surge, consumption recovery) and explain why it matters.
5. **One-sentence bottom line** — plain English verdict on where Argentina's real economy stands.

## Thresholds to flag explicitly
- GDP growth below 0%: recession territory
- GDP growth above 5%: strong recovery
- Investment (I_pct) below -10%: structural concern
- Exports (X_pct) above 10%: external sector strength
- Consumption (C_pct) diverging from GDP by more than 3pp: demand imbalance

## Tone
Be analytical, not descriptive. Compare current quarter to prior quarter and to the same quarter last year. Avoid hedging language — give a clear directional call. Keep it under 200 words.
